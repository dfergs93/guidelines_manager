# ACR BI-RADS — Breast Imaging Reporting & Data System

??? info "Reference Information"
    **Full Name:** Breast Imaging Reporting and Data System
    **Version:** 5th Edition (2013)
    **Publisher:** American College of Radiology (ACR)
    **Application:** Mammography, Breast Ultrasound, Breast MRI
    **Last Institutional Review:** January 2024
    **Modifications:** None

---

???+ abstract "BI-RADS Quick Reference"
    | Category | Assessment | Malignancy Risk | Action |
    |----------|------------|-----------------|--------|
    | **BI-RADS 0** | Incomplete | N/A | Additional imaging needed |
    | **BI-RADS 1** | Negative | Essentially 0% | Routine screening |
    | **BI-RADS 2** | Benign | Essentially 0% | Routine screening |
    | **BI-RADS 3** | Probably benign | ≤2% | 6-month short-interval follow-up |
    | **BI-RADS 4** | Suspicious | 2–95% | Tissue sampling (biopsy) |
    | **BI-RADS 4A** | Low suspicion | >2% to ≤10% | Biopsy |
    | **BI-RADS 4B** | Moderate suspicion | >10% to ≤50% | Biopsy |
    | **BI-RADS 4C** | High suspicion | >50% to <95% | Biopsy |
    | **BI-RADS 5** | Highly suggestive of malignancy | ≥95% | Biopsy |
    | **BI-RADS 6** | Known biopsy-proven malignancy | N/A | Surgical management |

## Detailed Classification

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   **Category 0: Incomplete**

    ---

    !!! warning "Additional Imaging Needed"
        Evaluation is incomplete. Additional imaging is necessary before a final category can be assigned.

        **Common reasons:**

        - Callback from screening mammography
        - Need for additional views (spot compression, magnification)
        - Prior study comparison needed
        - Ultrasound or MRI correlation required

    **Management:** Complete workup before final assessment

-   **Category 1: Negative**

    ---

    !!! success "Routine Screening"
        No abnormality found. Breast tissue is symmetric. No masses, architectural distortion, or suspicious calcifications.

    **Management:** Routine screening interval
    **Malignancy Risk:** Essentially 0%

-   **Category 2: Benign**

    ---

    !!! success "Routine Screening"
        Definite benign finding(s). The radiologist may describe a benign finding while still concluding there is no mammographic evidence of malignancy.

        **Examples:**

        - Involuting, calcified fibroadenoma
        - Multiple secretory calcifications
        - Oil cysts, lipomas, galactoceles, hamartomas
        - Simple cysts on ultrasound
        - Implants

    **Management:** Routine screening interval
    **Malignancy Risk:** Essentially 0%

-   **Category 3: Probably Benign**

    ---

    !!! info "Short-Interval Follow-Up"
        Finding has high probability of being benign. Not expected to change over the follow-up interval, but the radiologist would prefer to establish stability.

        **Qualifying lesions:**

        - **Mammography:** Non-calcified circumscribed solid mass, focal asymmetry, solitary group of punctate calcifications
        - **Ultrasound:** Complicated cyst, clustered microcysts, circumscribed solid mass with benign features
        - **MRI:** Non-mass enhancement with kinetics suggestive of benign etiology

    **Management:** Initial 6-month follow-up, then 12-month × 2 years
    **Malignancy Risk:** ≤2%

-   **Category 4: Suspicious**

    ---

    !!! warning "Tissue Sampling Recommended"
        Finding does not have classic benign morphology. Biopsy should be considered.

        **Subcategories:**

        - **4A:** Low suspicion — e.g., palpable solitary, well-defined solid mass on US; single dilated duct
        - **4B:** Moderate suspicion — e.g., partially circumscribed mass with ill-defined margin
        - **4C:** High suspicion — e.g., irregular, hypoechoic mass with angular margins; new cluster of fine pleomorphic calcifications

        !!! tip "Subcategory Guidance"
            Subcategorizing (4A/4B/4C) is **recommended** as it helps manage patient/clinician expectations for biopsy results.

    **Management:** Biopsy
    **Malignancy Risk:** 4A: >2–10% | 4B: >10–50% | 4C: >50–<95%

-   **Category 5: Highly Suggestive of Malignancy**

    ---

    !!! danger "Biopsy Required"
        Finding has a high probability (≥95%) of being malignant. Biopsy is required before any treatment.

        **Classic features:**

        - Spiculated high-density mass
        - Segmental/linear distribution of fine-linear or fine-linear branching (casting) calcifications
        - Irregular hypoechoic mass with spiculation, posterior shadowing, and angular margins on ultrasound

    **Management:** Tissue sampling (needle biopsy prior to definitive surgical management)
    **Malignancy Risk:** ≥95%

-   **Category 6: Known Biopsy-Proven Malignancy**

    ---

    !!! danger "Confirmed Malignancy"
        Used for imaging following biopsy that has shown malignancy, performed prior to definitive therapy.

        **Use cases:**

        - Pre-surgical planning imaging
        - Monitoring response to neoadjuvant chemotherapy
        - Post-biopsy MRI for extent of disease

    **Management:** Surgical/oncologic management
    **Malignancy Risk:** Known malignancy

</div>

## Mammography: Descriptor Lexicon

=== "Masses"

    | Feature | Benign Descriptors | Suspicious Descriptors |
    |---------|-------------------|----------------------|
    | **Shape** | Round, oval | Irregular |
    | **Margin** | Circumscribed | Obscured, microlobulated, indistinct, spiculated |
    | **Density** | Fat-containing, low density | High density |

=== "Calcifications"

    | Type | Description | Typical Assessment |
    |------|-------------|-------------------|
    | Skin calcifications | Polygonal, lucent center | BI-RADS 2 |
    | Vascular calcifications | Parallel tracks | BI-RADS 2 |
    | Coarse or popcorn | Large, dense | BI-RADS 2 |
    | Round/punctate | Smooth, well-defined, <1 mm | BI-RADS 2–3 |
    | Amorphous | Indistinct, cloudy | BI-RADS 4A |
    | Coarse heterogeneous | Irregular, 0.5–1 mm | BI-RADS 4B |
    | Fine pleomorphic | Irregular, <0.5 mm | BI-RADS 4B–4C |
    | Fine linear / fine linear branching | Casting, thin branching | BI-RADS 4C–5 |

    **Distribution descriptors (most to least suspicious):**
    Segmental → Linear → Regional → Grouped/Clustered → Diffuse

=== "Associated Features"

    - Architectural distortion
    - Asymmetry (focal, global, developing)
    - Intramammary lymph node
    - Skin/nipple lesion
    - Trabecular thickening
    - Axillary adenopathy

## Ultrasound: Descriptor Lexicon

| Feature | Benign | Suspicious |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Shape** | Oval, round | Irregular |
| **Orientation** | Parallel (wider than tall) | Not parallel (taller than wide) |
| **Margin** | Circumscribed | Not circumscribed (indistinct, angular, microlobulated, spiculated) |
| **Echo pattern** | Anechoic, hyperechoic, complex, heterogeneous | Hypoechoic |
| **Posterior features** | Enhancement | Shadowing |
| **Vascularity** | Absent or internal | Marked internal |

!!! tip "Simple Cyst Criteria (BI-RADS 2)"
    Anechoic + circumscribed + imperceptible wall + posterior enhancement → Simple cyst, no biopsy needed

## Breast MRI: BI-RADS Assessment

=== "Kinetic Curves"

    | Curve Type | Initial Phase | Delayed Phase | Significance |
    |------------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
    | **Type 1 (Persistent)** | Slow/moderate | Continued increase | Benign |
    | **Type 2 (Plateau)** | Medium/rapid | Plateau | Indeterminate |
    | **Type 3 (Washout)** | Rapid | Decrease | Suspicious |

=== "Enhancement Descriptors"

    **Focus:** <5 mm, too small to characterize
    **Mass:** 3D space-occupying lesion with or without margin, shape features
    **Non-mass enhancement (NME):** Enhancement without a discrete mass; classified by distribution and internal pattern

    | NME Distribution | Suspicion Level |
    |-----------------|-----------------|
    | Focal | Low |
    | Linear / ductal | High |
    | Segmental | High |
    | Regional | Moderate |
    | Diffuse | Low (often benign) |

## Background Parenchymal Enhancement (BPE)

!!! info "MRI — Background Parenchymal Enhancement Categories"
    | Category | Description |
    |----------|-------------|
    | **Minimal** | <25% of glandular tissue enhances |
    | **Mild** | 25–50% enhances |
    | **Moderate** | 50–75% enhances |
    | **Marked** | >75% enhances |

    Moderate/marked BPE can obscure lesions; consider timing relative to menstrual cycle (days 7–14 preferred).

## Breast Density Categories (Mammography)

| Category | Description | Clinical Significance |
|----------|-------------|----------------------|
| **A** | Almost entirely fatty | Lowest sensitivity limitation |
| **B** | Scattered areas of fibroglandular density | Mild sensitivity limitation |
| **C** | Heterogeneously dense | May obscure small masses; supplemental screening may be appropriate |
| **D** | Extremely dense | Highest sensitivity limitation; supplemental screening recommended |

!!! warning "Dense Breast Reporting"
    Many states require notification letters to patients with heterogeneously dense (C) or extremely dense (D) breasts regarding potential need for supplemental screening.

## Common Pitfalls

!!! warning "Avoid These Mistakes"

    **❌ Don't:**

    - Assign BI-RADS 3 to a screening mammogram without additional workup (category 0 should be assigned first)
    - Use BI-RADS 3 for lesions with >2% malignancy risk features
    - Forget to subcategorize BI-RADS 4 (4A/4B/4C)
    - Apply BI-RADS 6 before tissue sampling has confirmed malignancy

    **✅ Do:**

    - Complete diagnostic workup before assigning BI-RADS 3
    - Correlate mammography and ultrasound findings
    - Describe the most suspicious lesion when multiple are present
    - Include density assessment in every mammography report
